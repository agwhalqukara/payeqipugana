Early Life & Biography

Darci Lynne Farmer was born on October 12, 2004, in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma to her parents Misty and Clarke Farmer. She is the youngest of her three siblings. As a child, she was very shy, which hindered her performance.

In 2014, Farmer’s parents encouraged her to participate in the International Cinderella Scholarship Program. She was able to conquer her fears and shyness and became the International Mini Miss. During this competition, she met her mentor, Laryssa Bonacquisti, who suggested that she learn ventriloquism.

In the same year, she took part in the show Edmond’s Got Talent and won the first prize with just one-month preparation. Later, renowned ventriloquy coach Gary Owen agreed to coach her.

Career

Darci Lynne Farmer started her journey to stardom by winning the 2014 International Mini Miss. She became a household name when she appeared on the twelfth season of the talent show competition, America’s Got Talent in the year 2017.

Farmer’s first performance at the show was awe-inspiring, which led the judges to press the golden buzzer, sending her directly to the quarterfinals. Her performance was so brilliant that her video became viral on the internet and became the most viewed video with the first thirty hours in America’s Got Talent history. It also was the fourth most trending video of that year.

Farmer’s second performance was also equally amazing, and all the judges, as well as the live audience, gave her a standing ovation. She entered the top five after performing the song “Anything You Can Do.” Finally, she won the 12th season of the show, with a total of 52 million votes.

After winning the competition, she was seen in many television talk shows, including The Ellen DeGeneres Show, iHeartMedia, KJ103 Present Darci Lynne, and many more. She also appeared on the charity show, One Night With the Stars at The Garden or Madison Square Garden.

In 2017, Farmer went on a national tour which was named Darci Lynne and Friends Live in more than fifty locations. The following year, she hosted the television show, Darci Lynne: My Hometown Christmas, which aired on the NBC network.

In 2019, she participated in the spin-off of America’s Got Talent named, America’s Got Talent: The Champions. She went to the top three but was not able to win the show as they didn’t get enough votes.
