Early Beginnings and Education

Wu Qian‘s journey into the world of classical music began at a tender age in her hometown of Shanghai, China. Recognizing her potential early on, her parents enrolled her in the prestigious Shanghai Conservatory of Music when she was just nine years old.

Her exceptional talent soon earned her a scholarship to the Yehudi Menuhin School in the United Kingdom, where she studied under the guidance of renowned instructors like Irina Zaritskaya.

She didn’t stop there; she furthered her studies at the Royal Academy of Music in London, learning from masters such as Arnaldo Cohen and Christopher Elton. This solid educational foundation laid the groundwork for her illustrious career.

Performances and Recognition

At just fifteen, she showcased her prowess by performing Mozart’s Piano Concerto at the Queen Elizabeth Hall in London.

Her skill and stage presence were undeniable, leading to more opportunities such as her acclaimed performance at the Menuhin Festival in Gstaad, Switzerland.
